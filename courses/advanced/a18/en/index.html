A18 · Eval for creative output

Virality predictor, hook strength, retention risk, brand fit. When humans evaluate, when automation does. A/B in installations and client review.

What we’re building

An eval pipeline for creative output: measuring what’s usually “eyeballed.” The goal is a repeatable process for picking the best variant out of N, not “looked at the script, let’s go with this one.”

Middle (M11–M13) covered the eval base for code and text. Advanced is about eval of visual and audio-video creative.

Three evaluation tiers

TIER           WHAT WE MEASURE                  WHO / WHAT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
auto-metrics   tech compliance                   script
               (palette, resolution, fps,
                aspect ratio, taboo words)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
auto-AI eval   virality / brand fit /            AI services
               hook strength / retention         (Higgsfield
                                                  virality_predictor)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
human          artistic judgment,                client / art dir /
               client feedback                   review site

The tiers are sequential. Auto-metrics (cheap) filter technical defects. AI eval narrows top-N. The human makes the final call.

Auto-metrics: technical filter

What we measure without AI:

  • palette: colour histogram falls within the brand range (#0A0A0A ± 10, #C8FF00 ± 20);
  • sharpness: Laplacian variance — filters blurry frames;
  • aspect: strictly 4:5 / 9:16 / 16:9;
  • discrepancies: extra objects, ad plates (via CLIP zero-shot);
  • taboo words in captions: regex.
def auto_check(image_path: Path, brand: dict) -> list[str]:
    img = cv2.imread(str(image_path))
    errors = []
    if avg_color_diff(img, brand["palette"]) > 0.15:
        errors.append("palette off-brand")
    if cv2.Laplacian(img, cv2.CV_64F).var() < 50:
        errors.append("frame blurry")
    h, w = img.shape[:2]
    if abs((w/h) - brand["target_ar"]) > 0.05:
        errors.append(f"aspect {w/h:.2f}, expected {brand['target_ar']:.2f}")
    return errors

Auto-metrics filter, they don’t “evaluate.” They say “technically acceptable,” not “pick this one.”

Virality predictor

From production (skill higgsfield-generate with brain_activity): the Virality Predictor scores a clip across several axes:

  • hook strength — how strong the first 1–3 seconds are;
  • attention curve — where the audience’s eye sits;
  • retention risk — where the viewer loses interest;
  • distraction risk — competing visual elements;
  • creative score — aggregate.

Used in A18 as the AI-eval tier: pass top-N clips through virality, pick by hook strength and retention.

/higgsfield-generate brain_activity --video path/to/clip.mp4

Don’t turn this into the final decision — it’s a signal, not “the truth about virality.” Reality check — A/B against a real audience.

Brand fit

Brand fit is a separate AI eval: how well the frame/clip matches the brand tokens. Cheap option — CLIP with a brand-prefix prompt.

import torch, open_clip

model, _, preprocess = open_clip.create_model_and_transforms("ViT-B-32", pretrained="laion2b_s34b_b79k")
tokenizer = open_clip.get_tokenizer("ViT-B-32")

def brand_score(image: Image, brand_prompt: str) -> float:
    img_feat = model.encode_image(preprocess(image).unsqueeze(0))
    txt_feat = model.encode_text(tokenizer([brand_prompt]))
    return torch.cosine_similarity(img_feat, txt_feat).item()

brand_prompt — a short description of the visual language: “editorial high-contrast monochrome with single chartreuse accent, geometric, minimal.” Rank frames by score and cut the tail.

A/B in client review

From production (memory project_flag_review / project_svib_review): client review with a video gallery, likes, comments. The site template (flag-review) is reused per project.

Architecture:

  • static site on Cloudflare Pages under zavdv1;
  • Worker + KV for likes and comments;
  • 30 minutes to clone the template for a new project.

Use it as A/B: show 3 motion variants of one concept, the client votes/comments. The decision is fed by the feedback, not “I like this one.”

Old generations are not deleted, they’re moved to the archive (data-default-archived="true") — rule feedback_archive_never_delete. Design history transparency matters.

Real case: 12 posts → pick 4 finals

Setup: a series of 12 hero stills (see A5). Pick 4 for publication over a month.

Workflow:

  1. Auto-metrics on 12 frames → 1 rejected (Laplacian < 50, blurry). 11 remain.
  2. Brand fit via CLIP → rank top-8 by score > 0.32. 8 remain.
  3. Virality predictor on mp4 previews of each → rank by hook strength. Top-5.
  4. Client review: show 5 in flag-review, client comments. From 5, pick 4 for publication.

Selection time: 1 hour (most of it is the client review). Before the eval pipeline — 3–4 hours of “by eye” with back-and-forth.

When humans evaluate, when automation does

WhatWhoWhy
Tech compliance (aspect, colour, sharpness)ScriptObjective, cheap
Brand fitCLIP / AIMore objective than the eye, scales
Hook / retentionVirality AISimulates audience reaction
Artistic compositionArt directorNo AI replaces it yet
Final for the clientClientThe brand belongs to the client
Legal compliance (IP, faces)Lawyer / art dirCost of a mistake > cost of a check

Don’t try to automate “artistic composition” — AI eval shows patterns, not taste. Don’t try to eyeball “hook” — virality predictor is cheaper and on average more accurate.

Pitfalls

  • Virality eval ≠ virality — it’s a signal. Works well on comparisons, badly as an absolute metric.
  • CLIP brand-fit drifts on prompt-prefix — if the brand prefix changes between measurements, the scores aren’t comparable. Version the prompt.
  • Client votes for “the one like that other clip” — subjective preferences derail systematic selection. Structure the survey (axes hook / brand / message).
  • “We have X likes” — likes on a review site aren’t a virality metric; they’re 5–10 people’s engagement. Don’t pass it off as “a forecast.”

Recap

  • Three tiers: auto-metrics (filter) → AI eval (ranking) → human (final).
  • Virality predictor — a comparison signal, not “the truth about virality.”
  • Brand fit via CLIP — a scalable measure of brand match.
  • A/B client review — flag-review template, 30 minutes per client.
  • Don’t delete old variants — archive them.
